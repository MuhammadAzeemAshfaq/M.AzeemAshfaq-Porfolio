The Problem

Most fitness apps tell you what to do but not whether you are doing it right

People who work out at home or without a personal trainer often develop bad form without realising it. Over time this leads to injuries, slow progress, and eventually giving up. Existing apps offered workout plans but had no way to watch, correct, or motivate users in real time.

The client wanted a fitness app that felt less like a to-do list and more like having a coach and a community in your pocket.

My Approach

Breaking the project into three core pillars

Rather than trying to build everything at once, I broke the product into three focused areas and tackled each with a clear technical strategy.

  • The AI posture detection engine used the device camera with a pose estimation model to track joint positions in real time and compare them against reference form data, giving the user instant audio and visual feedback.
  • The AR challenge layer overlaid gamified workout targets onto the real world so users could complete exercises tied to their environment, making sessions feel different every day.
  • The community and leaderboard system tracked completed workouts, awarded points, and showed weekly rankings within friend groups and public tribes so users had a social reason to stay consistent.

The biggest technical challenge was running pose estimation on-device without draining the battery or causing lag. I optimised the model pipeline to process frames at 20fps on mid-range Android devices, which covered most of the target user base.

What I Learned

Key takeaways

On-device AI inference is very different from server-side inference. Getting the model small enough to run smoothly while keeping accuracy high required several rounds of quantisation and profiling. I came away with a much deeper understanding of mobile ML optimisation.

Gamification only works when the rewards feel earned. We went through three iterations of the leaderboard and points system before we found a balance that felt motivating rather than hollow. User testing at each stage was essential.
