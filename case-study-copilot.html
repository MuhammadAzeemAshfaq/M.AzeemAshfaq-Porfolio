AI Meeting Copilot

A real-time meeting assistant that transcribes, detects questions, and generates smart answers on the fly so you can focus on the conversation.

The Problem

Important meetings are stressful when you cannot keep up with both listening and responding

Whether it is a client presentation, a technical interview, or a board meeting, participants often struggle to listen carefully, take notes, and think of the right response all at the same time. People miss questions, forget key points, or give incomplete answers simply because the cognitive load is too high.

The client needed a tool that could sit quietly in the background during any meeting, follow the conversation in real time, and surface relevant information the moment it was needed without being distracting.

My Approach

Three real-time layers working together

The product was built around a real-time audio pipeline with three distinct processing layers running concurrently.

  • A live transcription layer that captured microphone audio and converted speech to text with low latency using a streaming speech-to-text model, displaying a rolling transcript on screen.
  • A question detection layer that analysed each new sentence as it was transcribed and classified it as a statement or a question using a fine-tuned NLP model, flagging questions with a visual highlight so the user could see them instantly.
  • An answer generation layer that took each detected question and retrieved relevant content from a context document the user uploaded before the meeting, then combined that with general knowledge to generate a concise suggested answer within a couple of seconds.

The hardest part was keeping end-to-end latency under two seconds from speech to suggested answer. Achieving this required careful pipeline architecture, streaming API calls, and running question detection client-side rather than server-side to eliminate a round trip.

What I Learned

Key takeaways

Real-time AI applications have a completely different set of constraints compared to standard request-response applications. Every millisecond matters and users notice even small delays. Building this project gave me a much deeper understanding of streaming APIs, WebSocket architecture, and how to design systems where multiple async processes need to stay in sync.

Context quality matters more than model quality. The most impactful improvement to answer relevance came from improving how the uploaded document was chunked and indexed, not from swapping to a bigger model.
